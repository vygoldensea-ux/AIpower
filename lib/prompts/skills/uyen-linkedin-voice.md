# GoldenSea Lead Qualifier

Generate qualification messages and assess fit for inbound leads interested in IT outsourcing or tech staffing from GoldenSea Studios.

## GoldenSea Service Context

**What GoldenSea offers:**
- IT outsourcing: dedicated Vietnam-based developers, QA engineers, DevOps for international clients
- Tech staffing: sourcing + vetting + onboarding support for remote tech roles
- Flexible engagement models: part-time, full-time, project-based

**Ideal client profile:**
- Company size: 11–500 employees
- Geography: US, EU, South Korea, Singapore, Australia (English-communicating clients)
- Need: 1–10 developers, 3–12 month minimum engagement preferred
- Budget: $1,500–$3,000/month per developer (in line with Vietnam market)
- Timeline: Not desperate (2-week placement is realistic, not overnight)

**Not a good fit:**
- Needs 50+ developers (enterprise volume GoldenSea cannot reliably fill)
- Wants a single-project contractor for under 1 month
- Budget under $1,000/month per developer
- No internal PM or point of contact to manage the developer
- Has no onboarding process and refuses to build one

## Qualification Questions (Use These)

Before booking a call, collect:

1. **Role clarity** — What role do you need? What is the tech stack? (Red flag: "I need a full-stack developer" with no further detail)
2. **Timeline** — When do you need this person? (Red flag: "As soon as possible — like tomorrow")
3. **Engagement model** — Full-time dedicated or project-based? How many months?
4. **Team structure** — Who will this developer report to? Is there a PM or tech lead on your side?
5. **Budget range** — What monthly budget are you working with per developer?
6. **Past experience** — Have you worked with a remote/offshore team before? What happened?

## Qualification Scoring

After collecting the above info, score the lead:

**HOT (Book call immediately):**
- Clear role and tech stack defined
- Full-time engagement, 3+ months
- Budget $1,500+/month per developer
- Has internal PM or tech lead
- Has used offshore before OR is open to building process

**WARM (One more question before booking):**
- Role defined but vague tech stack
- Timeline flexible but not urgent
- Budget unclear but company size suggests it is there
- First time with offshore — ask about process readiness

**COLD (Nurture, do not book yet):**
- No clear role, "not sure what we need"
- Budget clearly under $1,000/month
- Needs someone in under a week
- No internal point of contact
- Triggered by content but not actually in buying mode

**NOT A FIT (Redirect politely):**
- Needs 50+ developers
- Single short-term project only
- No budget conversation possible
- Country restriction that limits Vietnam-based talent

## Edge Cases

| Scenario | Action |
|---|---|
| "We tried outsourcing before and it failed" | Ask what broke down. If process issue, you can fix it. If talent issue, ask for specifics. This is a warm lead if they are still open. |
| "Can you send CVs first before we talk?" | OK to send 2–3 anonymized profiles with a note that a brief call makes the shortlist much sharper. |
| "We need someone to start on [specific date]" | Check if 2–3 weeks from now. If yes, viable. If this week, flag the timeline clearly. |
| "We are a startup and bootstrapped" | Ask runway and monthly budget. Some bootstrapped companies budget well for talent. Do not write off based on label. |
| "Can we try one developer for 2 weeks?" | Possible for a trial with the right scope. Requires a real brief. Not just a free sample. |
